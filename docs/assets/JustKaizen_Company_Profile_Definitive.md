# JustKaizen AI: Company Profile (Definitive Spec)

## 1. Company Overview

**Company:** JustKaizen AI
**What they do:** Enterprise AI platform that automates business operations, analytics, and decision-making workflows for mid-market and enterprise organizations.
**Mission:** Build intelligent automation that makes every team operate at their best.
**Founded:** 2019, remote-first (HQ in San Francisco for legal/investor purposes)
**Current state (Q1 2026):** 1,200 active employees, ~$180M ARR, 25% YoY growth, preparing for IPO in 2027
**Data scope:** January 2021 through March 2026 (5 years, 63 months)
**Total employees ever:** ~1,900 (1,200 active + ~700 terminated across the data scope)

## 4. 2024 Q1 Layoff Details

150 employees terminated as "Reduction in Force" in Q1 2024. Driven by market correction, over-hiring during 2023 hypergrowth, and board pressure to demonstrate path to profitability ahead of IPO.

| Department | People Cut | % of Dept at Time | Notes |
|------------|-----------|-------------------|-------|
| Engineering | 40 | 10% | Junior roles, consolidated duplicate platform/infrastructure teams |
| Sales | 35 | 18% | SDR team downsized, underperforming vertical teams cut |
| Marketing | 20 | 20% | Brand, events, and employer branding teams reduced |
| G&A | 18 | 17% | Facilities, internal comms, duplicate admin roles |
| Customer Success | 15 | 12% | Tier-1 support reduced, implementation team consolidated |
| People | 12 | 15% | 4 recruiters, 3 L&D, 2 DEIB, 2 People Ops, 1 Total Rewards |
| Product | 10 | 10% | UX Research consolidated, junior PM roles |
| Data & Analytics | 0 | 0% | Protected. Small team, fully utilized. |

**Post-layoff narrative:** Company stabilized through Q2-Q3 2024, secured Series D funding ($150M at $2.5B valuation) in Q3 2024, and resumed disciplined hiring. By Q4 2025, headcount returned to pre-layoff levels. IPO targeted for 2027.

## 8. Compensation Structure

### 8a. Job Family Market Multipliers

Compensation bands vary by job family, not just by level. An IC4 Staff Engineer and an IC4 Staff People Ops Specialist are the same level but in different markets.

| Job Family | Departments | Multiplier vs Base | Rationale |
|------------|------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Technical | Engineering, Data Engineering (D&A) | 1.20-1.25x | AI/ML premium, competitive market |
| Technical-Adjacent | Product, Design, UX, Product Analytics, Data Science, BI | 1.10x | Technical skills, slightly less competitive |
| Commercial | Sales (AE, Enterprise, Sales Eng) | 1.00x base + OTE | Quota-carrying roles have variable comp |
| Commercial-Support | SDR | 0.85x base + OTE | Entry-level quota roles |
| Commercial-Adjacent | Customer Success, Implementation | 0.90-0.95x | Customer-facing, not quota-carrying |
| Marketing | Marketing (all sub-depts) | 0.92-1.00x | Growth pays more, Content/Events less |
| Operations | G&A (Finance, IT, Facilities, BizOps) | 0.80-0.92x | Exception: Legal at 1.10x |
| People | People / HR (all sub-depts) | 0.80-0.95x | Total Rewards and HRBPs higher, L&D lower |

### 8c. Historical Band Adjustments

Bands shift upward over time to reflect market adjustments. When generating historical comp records, apply the multiplier for the year the comp was set.

| Year | Multiplier | Interpretation |

| 2020 | 0.82x | Series A, still below market |
| 2021 | 0.85x | Series B, approaching market |
| 2022 | 0.88x | Hypergrowth, competitive but lagging |
| 2023 | 0.92x | Market correction, bands catching up |
| 2024 | 0.95x | Post-RIF, significant market adjustment (Sept 2024) |

### 8d. Compensation Change Rules

| Change Type | Typical Increase | Trigger |
|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| Promotion | 10-15% | Must land within the new level's band |
| Annual merit (Significantly Exceeds / Exceeds) | 4-5% | Top performer, January each year |
| Annual merit (Meets Expectations) | 3-4% | Standard performer, January each year |
| Annual merit (Partially Meets) | 0-1% | Below expectations |
| Annual merit (Does Not Meet) | 0% | Comp frozen |
| Market Adjustment | 5-8% | Company-wide, occurred September 2024 post-Series D |
| Lateral move | 0% | No comp change for same-level department transfers |

**Compa-ratio formula:** `ROUND(salary / comp_band_mid, 2)`

**Annual review eligibility:** Employee must have at least 6 months tenure by January 15 of the review year.

## 9. Performance Rating System

### Rating Scale

| Rating Numeric | Rating Description | Target Distribution |
|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 5 | Significantly Exceeds Expectations | 5-8% |
| 4 | Exceeds Expectations | 20-25% |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | 50-55% |
| 2 | Partially Meets Expectations | 10-15% |
| 1 | Does Not Meet Expectations | 2-5% |

### Review Cycles

- **Frequency:** Semi-annual (Mid-Year and Year-End)
- **Cycle names:** "2021 Mid-Year", "2021 Year-End", "2022 Mid-Year", etc.
- **Review period:** Mid-Year covers Jan-Jun, Year-End covers Jul-Dec
- **Completion window:** 4-6 weeks after period end

### Top Performer Flag

An employee is flagged as a Top Performer ("Y") if:
- Latest performance rating >= 4 (Exceeds or Significantly Exceeds), OR
- is_critical_talent = TRUE

Otherwise Top Performer = "N".

### Involuntary Termination Reasons (Attrition-Eligible)

These are performance-driven or conduct-driven terminations that count toward attrition metrics. They represent roughly 4-5% of all non-RIF terminations.

| Reason | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Performance | Terminated for sustained underperformance (scores 4-5). Not regrettable. |
| Misconduct | Terminated for behavioral violations. Not regrettable. |
| Policy Violation | Terminated for policy breaches. Not regrettable. |

### Excluded Termination Reasons

**Note: This is a data cleaning convention applied during data preparation, not a dashboard-level filter.** These termination reasons are removed from the attrition calculation population because they represent structural or operational workforce changes, not true employee-initiated or performance-driven attrition. Removing them produces attrition rates that accurately reflect the retention dynamics the People team can actually influence.

Excluded reasons:

- Reduction in Force
- End of Contract
- Entity Change
- Acquisition/Merger
- End of Internship
- International Transfer
- Relocation (involuntary, company-initiated)
- Converting to FT (contractor conversion)

### New Hire Flag

- Active employees: "New Hire" if `DATE_DIFF(report_month_end, hire_date, DAY) <= 365`
- Terminated employees: "New Hire" if `DATE_DIFF(termination_date, hire_date, DAY) <= 365`
- Otherwise: "Tenured"

### Tenure Buckets

1-year intervals. Boundary rule: exactly N years falls into the higher bucket (exclusive lower bound, inclusive upper bound).

| Bucket | Range |
|--------|-------|
| 0-1 Years | 0 < tenure_months <= 12 |
| 1-2 Years | 12 < tenure_months <= 24 |
| 2-3 Years | 24 < tenure_months <= 36 |
| 3-4 Years | 36 < tenure_months <= 48 |
| 4-5 Years | 48 < tenure_months <= 60 |
| 5+ Years | tenure_months > 60 |

## 13. Engagement Survey Design

### Survey Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Frequency | Semi-annual (H1 and H2) |
| Cycles | 2021-H1 through 2025-H2 (10 cycles) |
| Anonymization | Department-level minimum. Sub-department included when response count >= 5. |
| Questions | 27 questions across 8 themes |
| Response rate | 78-88% per cycle |

### Engagement Themes and Questions

Each question is scored on a 1-5 Likert scale (1 = Strongly Disagree, 5 = Strongly Agree). eNPS is scored separately on a 0-10 scale.

**ENG - Employee Engagement (4 questions)**
- ENG1: "I would recommend this company as a great place to work." (eNPS proxy)
- ENG2: "I see myself still working here two years from now."
- ENG3: "I feel motivated to give my best effort every day."
- ENG4: "I am proud to be part of this organization."

**MGE - Manager Effectiveness (4 questions)**
- MGE1: "My manager gives me actionable feedback that helps me improve."
- MGE2: "My manager genuinely cares about my career development."
- MGE3: "My manager makes fair and consistent decisions."
- MGE4: "I feel comfortable bringing up problems or concerns with my manager."

**CGD - Career Growth & Development (3 questions)**
- CGD1: "I can see a realistic path for advancing my career here."
- CGD2: "I have access to the learning opportunities I need to grow."
- CGD3: "The work I do is helping me build skills that matter for my future."

**WLB - Work-Life Balance (3 questions)**
- WLB1: "I can sustain my current workload without burning out."
- WLB2: "I feel supported when I need to take time away from work."
- WLB3: "The expectations for my availability outside of working hours are reasonable."

**REC - Recognition (3 questions)**
- REC1: "I receive meaningful recognition when I do great work."
- REC2: "Recognition at this company is based on merit, not politics."
- REC3: "My contributions are visible to people beyond my immediate team."

**CUL - Culture & Values (3 questions)**
- CUL1: "I feel like I belong at this company."
- CUL2: "The company lives its values in how decisions are actually made."
- CUL3: "I trust senior leadership to act in the best interest of employees."

**COM - Communication & Transparency (3 questions)**
- COM1: "I understand how my work connects to the company's top priorities."
- COM2: "Leadership communicates important changes clearly and honestly."
- COM3: "Teams across the company collaborate effectively."

**RES - Resources & Enablement (3 questions)**
- RES1: "I have the tools and systems I need to do my job well."
- RES2: "I have enough context and information to make good decisions in my role."
- RES3: "Our team's processes help me do my best work, rather than getting in the way."
